Temporary Staffing

You need someone physically there to repair it when there is a problem with your hardware. Say, for instance, your system won't boot; there's obviously no way to fix that issue over the phone, a technician will need to be physically present to look at the hardware. Rather of possibly waiting hours for a technician to arrive, diagnose, and repair the problem, you might solve the hardware issue in minutes simply by getting someone in-house. Getting in-house help may also contribute to a more reliable diagnosis because a person on-site can spot things that you may think are unrelated but may actually be a key contributor to the problem.

Having a dedicated on-site technician to assist with any problem, no matter how small it can make you more likely to ask for assistance. Calling the tech support company frequently might make you feel like a nuisance (though that's not necessarily the case and tech support is happy to help!) and getting an individual on-site will help relieve you of that worry. Moreover, asking about minor issues when they occur could help avoid major problems down the line, so having your on-site support look into even the smallest issues could help prevent future outages.

Trying to express a computer's noise, or the pattern in which a screen flashed is difficult, especially over the phone or in an email. To have someone physically present makes explaining the problem simpler, or, better still, telling them in person what the problem is. This can lead to a much more accurate diagnosis.
